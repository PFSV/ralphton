# M15–M17 — Figure 4: the LLM-trained RCNN

Status: **C14 and C16 reproduced (ROI-level). C13, C17, C18 not reproducible from released
assets. C15 pending (probe running).**

Drivers: `experiments/fig4_dnn.py`, `experiments/fig4b_probe.py`,
`experiments/verify_fig4_pipeline.py`
Results: `reports/figures/fig4_dnn.json`
Runs: `logs/fig4_center10.log`, `logs/verify_fig4.log`

No training was required. All 20 checkpoints (10 seeds × {mpnet, multihot}) are published, so
this module re-extracts activations from the **authors' own weights**. That is a strength — it
removes training variance as an explanation for any mismatch — and a limitation: it means we
verify their *analysis*, not their *training*.

---

## 0. What this module does NOT test — read this before the numbers

The driver originally printed its table under the header "Fig. 4c/4d". **That was wrong**, and
an adversarial audit of the code path caught it (4 of 21 candidate defects survived; §3).

Figures 4c and 4d are **volumetric searchlight contrast maps** — a 6-voxel-radius sphere at
every location in `func1pt8mm` space, RSA inside each sphere, then the difference between two
models, then a t-test with BH-FDR across ~200k vertices (`nsd_searchlight_main_tf.py:24-26`;
`py_plot_brain_utils.py:134`). We have **no searchlight implementation**, and the volumetric
betas it needs were never downloaded (`nsd_data.py:42-44` — we built `fsaverage` only).

What we compute instead is the **streams-ROI, fsaverage RSA**. This is not a substitute we
invented: it is the authors' own ROI adjudication of the same two contrasts
(`examples/roi_analyses.py`, `PAPER_FIG == 'fig4'`, pushing exactly these 20 checkpoints), and
it is what **Fig. 4e** plots. So:

| | testable here? |
|---|---|
| C14/C16 as **directional** claims ("RCNN aligns better than X") | **yes** |
| C14/C16 as **spatial** claims ("across a wide network of higher visual areas", p. 1224) | **no** — four ROI means cannot localise anything |

The table below is therefore labelled **Fig. 4e**, and the spatial half of C14/C16 is recorded
as **not attempted**, not as reproduced.

10 seeds, centre crop, mean over 8 participants. Correction is plain division by the LOSO
noise ceiling on the shared 515 images (same convention as Fig. 3; release:
`DO_NOISE_CEILING=True`, `nsd_roi_analyses.py:162`).

| model | early | ventral | lateral | parietal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LLM-trained RCNN** | 0.2007 | **0.4289** | **0.4696** | **0.3931** |
| category-trained RCNN (control) | 0.1928 | 0.3564 | 0.3652 | 0.3424 |
| MPNet embedding (the RCNN's own training target) | 0.1630 | 0.3499 | 0.4035 | 0.3583 |

Noise ceilings (mean over subjects): early 0.528, ventral 0.723, lateral 0.474, parietal 0.377.

### C16 — the LLM-trained RCNN beats the matched category-trained control

Holds in **all four ROIs** and **8/8 participants**. The seed distributions do not even
overlap: the LLM arm's *worst* seed (mean r 0.1882) beats the category arm's *best* (0.1776).

| ROI | Δr (LLM − category) | p (paired) | p (unpaired, *as released*) |
|---|---|---|---|
| early | +0.0079 | 1.0e-02 | 0.748 **n.s.** |
| ventral | +0.0725 | 3.8e-07 | 3.2e-07 |
| lateral | +0.1044 | 6.6e-09 | 2.0e-05 |
| parietal | +0.0507 | 2.7e-05 | 2.6e-04 |

This is a genuine reproduction of the paper's headline modelling claim, on the authors' own
weights, with the correct statistical test.

**Note the `early` column.** The released `ttest_ind` calls EVC null for *both* claims
(p = 0.28, p = 0.75) where the correct paired test finds an effect (p = 8.7e-05, p = 0.010).
This is a concrete, self-contained demonstration that the released test is not merely
theoretically wrong but *materially* wrong — it flips conclusions on our data. (It does **not**
license any claim about what it did to *their* numbers; see the retraction in
`M6_M7_M8_M9_fig3.md` §0.)

## 2. The floor nobody computes — and why the `early` row is nearly meaningless

`verify_fig4_pipeline.py` V3 runs the **same architecture with random weights**.

| ROI | trained | random init | ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| early | 0.1077 | 0.0677 | **1.59×** |
| ventral | 0.3364 | 0.0158 | 21.3× |
| lateral | 0.2559 | 0.0075 | 34.0× |
| parietal | 0.1678 | 0.0132 | 12.7× |

(raw r, subj01, centre crop.)

An **untrained** RCNN already reaches r = 0.068 in early visual cortex — 63% of what the
trained one achieves. In EVC, Fig. 4 is largely measuring the *architecture's* image statistics
(convolution + pooling + layer norm), not the LLM training objective. In ventral and lateral it
is not: training buys a 21–34× improvement, and that is where the paper's claims live.

Neither the paper nor the released code computes this floor. It costs one GPU-hour. Any future
version of this figure should print it next to every bar — a reader cannot otherwise tell how
much of a bar is architecture.

This does not weaken C14/C16 (both are *differences between trained nets*, and both are largest
exactly where the floor is lowest). It weakens any reading of the **absolute** EVC numbers.

## 3. Audit of our own pipeline — what we checked before trusting the table

A 4-lens adversarial audit (RSA estimator, preprocessing, checkpoint/layer, claim mapping) with
3 skeptics per finding produced 21 candidates; 4 survived. Three are fixed above (label, noise
ceiling, C18's status). The rest of the checks:

**V1 — are the released weights actually in the graph?** The driver's only guard was
`assert act.shape[1] == 512`, which a randomly-initialised net passes. We now compare the raw
HDF5 checkpoint against the built graph: the first conv kernel `(7,7,3,32)` is **bit-identical**
in layer `RCL_0_BConv`, and a freshly-initialised net does **not** match. **PASS.** We are not
measuring Keras's initialiser.

**V2 — the resize path.** The release never feeds the net the 425×425 NSD stimuli. It reads
`ms_coco_embeddings_square256.h5` (`get_dnn_activities.py:11`) — images already resampled to
256 — so its path is 425→256→128 and ours is 425→128. **That file is a private cluster path and
was never published.** We measured the consequence instead of arguing about it: the RDM
correlation between the two resize paths is **0.9917** (per-image feature cosine 0.9909).
Immaterial to anything we report. Recorded as a reproduction risk that turned out to be inert.

**Seed estimator (a candidate defect we *rejected*).** One finder argued we invert the paper's
seed aggregation, citing the Fig. 4c caption: "RCNN RDMs are averaged across ten network
instances". The Methods say the opposite, and the Methods win — "we compute individual RDMs for
each seed and then average correlations with brain data across seeds" — and *every* code path in
the release averages correlations, never RDMs (`nsd_roi_analyses_figure.py:101-103`;
`roi_wise_scatter.py:111`). Our estimator is correct. **The paper's caption contradicts its own
Methods**; logged as discrepancy X9.

C18 is a control the paper leans on to rule out the alternative that the LLM arm merely wins by
having a richer output space. It cannot be checked by anyone outside the authors' cluster.

## 5. Honest accounting of this module's own process

- The audit lost **33 verifier agents to a session limit**. My `survives = upheld >= 2` rule then
  filed the resulting **0-of-0** findings as *refuted* when they had simply never been checked.
  I re-verified the one with real numeric stakes (V2, the resize) by direct measurement. The
  others are listed in the workflow output as unverified, not as cleared.
- Two earlier scares were my own instrumentation, not the science: the driver printed nothing
  until its final table (so a stall and a healthy run looked identical), and several `wait`
  commands used `pgrep -f` patterns that matched their own command line and blocked forever.
  Both fixed (commit `7d0f894`; PID-based waits).
