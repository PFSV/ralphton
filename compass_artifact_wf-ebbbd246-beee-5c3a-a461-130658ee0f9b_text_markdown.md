# 문헌 조사: TFM 사전학습 prior를 위한 "LLM 에이전트 기반 실세계 모사 tabular 데이터 생성기"

## TL;DR (핵심 요약)
- **당신의 정확한 교차점을 차지한 논문은 없습니다** — "LLM 에이전트가 의미론(semantics)·현실성(realism)을 반영한 tabular 생성기를 설계하고, 그 출력이 tabular foundation model(TFM)의 *사전학습 prior*가 되는" 연구는 아직 없어 핵심 아이디어의 novelty는 방어 가능합니다. 다만 반드시 선제적으로 구분해야 할 "니어미스(nearest-miss)" 논문이 둘 있습니다: Hod, Rosenblatt & Stoyanovich (arXiv 2504.14368) — LLM 에이전트로 스키마 메타데이터에서 SCM을 구축하지만 용도가 DP(차등 프라이버시) 분류기 사전학습이고 TFM이 아님; O'PRIOR (arXiv 2605.18971) — TFM 사전학습용 현실성 지향 조합형 SCM prior를 설계하지만 LLM을 전혀 쓰지 않음.
- 가장 강한 포지셔닝은 **역할 (b): "LLM 에이전트 = 생성기 설계자/구성자"** (SCM/DAG 구조, 컬럼 의미론, 분포 파라미터를 에이전트가 결정하고 샘플링은 프로그램이 수행)입니다. 역할 (a)의 치명적 반론들 — OpenML 데이터셋에 대한 LLM 암기/오염(Bordt et al.), 프라이버시 누출(Ward et al.), 사전학습 규모에서의 토큰 비용 — 을 구조적으로 회피하기 때문입니다.
- 리뷰어는 TabPFN v2, TabICL/TabICLv2, Mitra, 그리고 ("현실성 논쟁"용) TabForestPFN과의 비교를 요구할 것이고, **"현실성이 중요하지 않을 수 있다"**는 반론을 제기할 것입니다. TabForestPFN은 "fine-tuned TabForest가 WhyTrees에서 최고, fine-tuned TabPFN이 TabZilla에서 우세"라는 결과, 즉 비현실적이지만 복잡한 데이터가 fine-tuning 후 이길 수 있음을 보였습니다. 논문은 Mitra의 "다양성/구별성/성능" prior 품질 프레임워크와 Real-TabPFN/TabDPT의 "real 데이터가 도움된다" 증거를 반드시 다뤄야 합니다.

## 핵심 발견
1. **필드의 초점이 아키텍처에서 prior 설계로 이동했습니다.** Mitra가 이를 명시적으로 선언합니다 — "합성 데이터셋을 생성하는 분포, 즉 데이터 prior의 설계에 더 큰 관심을 기울여야 한다" — 그리고 최초의 체계적 prior 품질 방법론(Generalizability Matrix G, Performance Vector P)을 제공합니다. 당신 논문이 진입하는 지적 공간이 바로 여기입니다.
2. **역할 (b)의 직접적 방법론 선행 연구가 존재합니다.** Hod et al. (2504.14368)은 이미 스키마 메타데이터만으로 SCM(변수 → DAG → 구조방정식 → Pyro 코드)을 도출하는 LLM 에이전트를 갖고 있습니다. novelty는 이 논문과의 대비로 조각해야 합니다: 그들은 타깃 스키마당 하나의 surrogate를 만들어 *DP 분류기* 사전학습에 쓰지만, 당신은 *TFM prior* 역할을 할 *데이터셋들의 분포*를 생성합니다.
3. **타깃(TFM prior) 쪽의 직접 선행 연구도 존재합니다.** O'PRIOR는 TFM 사전학습 전용으로 현실성 지향 조합형 SCM prior를 설계했지만 — 전적으로 프로그램 방식이고, LLM도 의미론도 없습니다. 당신의 "prior 설계" 경쟁자입니다.
4. **의미론 공백은 실재하며 문서화되어 있습니다.** ConTextTab은 컬럼 이름과 의미 있는 값들이 TabPFN/TabICL에서 사용되지 않음을 명시합니다; CARTE/TabSTAR/TARTE는 의미론을 *real* 데이터로 주입할 뿐, LLM이 설계한 *합성* prior로 주입한 적은 없습니다. 이 교차점은 비어 있습니다.
5. **"현실성이 중요한가?" 논쟁은 미결이며 핵심 쟁점입니다.** TabForestPFN은 비현실적이지만 복잡한 결정경계가 (fine-tuning 후) 도움이 된다고 주장; Real-TabPFN과 TabDPT는 real 데이터가 도움이 된다고 주장; Mitra는 충실도보다 다양성+구별성이 중요하다고 주장합니다. 논문은 여기서 입장을 취해야 합니다.

## Details

### Category 1 — Directly Relevant / Closest Work (novelty-critical)

**Do You Really Need Public Data? Surrogate Public Data for Differential Privacy on Tabular Data** — Shlomi Hod, Lucas Rosenblatt, Julia Stoyanovich, 2025, arXiv:2504.14368. A multi-step LLM **agent** (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Llama 3.3 70B) elicits a structural causal model from schema-level metadata only: it lists variables, proposes constraints, selects exogenous/root nodes, proposes DAG edges (verified acyclic), maps each variable to a structural equation over its parents, assigns distributional parameters, and auto-compiles to runnable Pyro code that samples data. A "panel of experts" ensemble mixes multiple runs. *Relevance:* the closest existing instantiation of your role (b). Distinguish sharply: they build ONE surrogate per target schema to pretrain a DP FTTransformer before DP-SGD fine-tuning — not a TFM prior, not a distribution over many datasets, and per-dataset rather than a general prior. **This is the single most important paper to pre-empt in related work.**

**LLM-Driven Performance-Space Augmentation for Meta-Learning-Based Algorithm Selection** — Darren Zhu, Daren Ler, 2026, arXiv:2605.09518. Prompts an LLM to generate whole synthetic regression datasets, steered toward target regions of a 2-D performance space defined by cross-validated R² of two landmarker algorithms, to augment a meta-dataset for algorithm selection. Evaluated on 42 UCI regression datasets + 730 synthetic datasets; uniform augmentation improved subset accuracy substantially. *Relevance:* Overlaps on "LLM generates whole synthetic tabular datasets to enrich a meta-learning corpus," but the objective is performance-space coverage (not real-world semantic mimicry) and the consumer is a meta-learner, not a PFN. Easy to distinguish.

**Incorporating LLM Priors into Tabular Learners** — Max Zhu et al., 2023, arXiv:2311.11628. Uses LLM prior knowledge to inform tabular classifiers (interpretable, controllable); notes integrating LLM priors into TabPFN-style models as future work. *Relevance:* an early gesture toward "LLM priors + tabular," but not generative and not for pretraining.

**Verdict on competitors:** No single paper found (arXiv/OpenReview/Semantic Scholar, 2024–2026) simultaneously (A) uses an LLM agent to design a semantic/realistic tabular generator AND (B) targets a TFM/PFN pretraining prior. Hod et al. occupies (A) not (B); O'PRIOR and Mitra occupy (B) not (A). The intersection is your defensible novelty.

### Category 2 — Synthetic Prior Design for TFMs (baseline tradition)

- **TabPFN v1** — Hollmann, Müller, Eggensperger, Hutter, ICLR 2023. Transformer pretrained on millions of synthetic datasets from an SCM prior; single-forward-pass Bayesian in-context inference for small tabular classification.
- **TabPFN v2** — Hollmann et al., Nature 637(8045):319–326, 2025 (doi:10.1038/s41586-024-08328-6). Extends to regression; the target regime is small/medium tables (the Nature paper targets datasets with up to 10,000 samples and up to 500 features — the "small-dataset" regime that constitutes 36 of TabArena's 51 datasets); enriched priors with more sophisticated DAG construction. **TabPFN-2.5** (arXiv:2511.08667) is the latest iteration and current SOTA claim.
- **TabICL** — Qu, Holzmüller, Varoquaux, Le Morvan, ICML 2025, arXiv:2502.05564. Scales ICL to large tables (pretrained on synthetic datasets with up to 60K samples; handles 500K at inference) via a two-stage column-then-row attention architecture; adds a tree-based component to the prior (idea credited to Léo Grinsztajn). **TabICLv2** — arXiv:2602.11139 — a better/faster/scalable open TFM with a useful related-work taxonomy of PFN-style priors (SCM, tree, mixed, hierarchical).
- **Mitra** — Zhang et al. (Amazon/AutoGluon), 2025, arXiv:2510.21204, NeurIPS 2025. Curated mixture of SCM + tree-based (gradient boosting, random forest, decision tree) priors; introduces prior-quality criteria: performance on real data, diversity, distinctiveness (Generalizability Matrix G, Performance Vector P). *Relevance:* provides the exact evaluation vocabulary and motivation your paper builds on.
- **LimiX** — Zhang et al., 2025, arXiv:2509.03505. Hierarchical SCM prior via three-stage DAG construction (local causal structures → full DAG → sampling → task adaptation) with controllable difficulty/solvability; unified model for classification, regression, imputation, feature/sample selection, causal inference. **LimiX-2M** (arXiv:2606.04485) addresses low-rank collapse/attention bottlenecks.
- **TabForestPFN / den Breejen et al.** — "Fine-tuned In-Context Learning Transformers are Excellent Tabular Data Classifiers," 2024, arXiv:2405.13396 (OpenReview pE0UM18TQh). A forest generator (overfit decision trees used as data-generating processes) makes *unrealistic but complex-decision-boundary* datasets. Key finding: "TabForest outperforms TabPFN on some real-world datasets when fine-tuning, despite having lower zero-shot performance due to the unrealistic nature of the pretraining datasets"; and "Fine-tuned TabForest is the best on WhyTrees, while fine-tuned TabPFN is favored on TabZilla." TabForestPFN mixes both generators. *Relevance:* the central "realism doesn't matter (much)" counter-evidence you must engage.
- **TabDPT** — Ma et al., 2024. Pretrained on *real* data with in-context retrieval + self-supervised masked-column modeling; shows large-scale real-data pretraining is competitive and extends to regression.
- **Real-TabPFN** — Garg, …, Hollmann, Müller, Hutter, 2025, arXiv:2507.03971. Continued pretraining of TabPFNv2 on a curated collection of 71 large real-world tables from OpenML/Kaggle; achieves substantial gains on 29 datasets from the OpenML AutoML Benchmark, and shows that this curated set "yields superior downstream predictive accuracy compared to using broader, potentially noisier corpora like CommonCrawl or GitTables."
- **O'PRIOR (Shaping the Prior: How Synthetic Task Distributions Determine Tabular Foundation Model Quality)** — Bouadi et al. (Lexsi Labs), 2026, arXiv:2605.18971. Compositional realism prior for TFM pretraining: hierarchical SCM over five mechanism families (MLPs, trees, 1-D convolutions, RBF GPs, VARs), realism engine (heterogeneous marginals, MCAR/MAR/MNAR missingness, target transforms), stress module (confounding, spurious correlations/shortcuts, covariate shift), leakage-safe curriculum; architecture/optimizer/compute held fixed, only the prior varied. *Relevance:* your closest "designed realistic prior for TFM" competitor — but programmatic, no LLM, no semantics.
- **APT (Adversarially Pre-trained Transformer)** — 2025, arXiv:2502.04573. Adversarial synthetic-data "agents" shift their DGP to challenge the model, yielding more diverse priors than TabPFN. *Relevance:* note the word "agents" here means adversarial data generators, NOT LLM agents — a lexical false positive to disambiguate.
- **Related PFN-prior analyses:** Drift-Resilient TabPFN (two-level generative prior modulating SCM parameters over time); FairPFN (arXiv:2506.07049, causal-fairness prior); "Causal Pre-training Under the Fairness Lens" (arXiv:2601.17912); "Does TabPFN Understand Causal Structures?" (arXiv:2511.07236). These probe *why* SCM priors work and are useful for your "why semantic/realistic priors could help" argument.

### Category 3 — LLM-Based Tabular Data Generation (general)

- **GReaT** — Borisov et al., ICLR 2023, arXiv:2210.06280. Fine-tunes an autoregressive LLM (GPT-2/DistilGPT-2) on serialized rows ("Col is Val…") with random feature-order permutation for arbitrary conditioning. Foundational LLM-tabular generator.
- **GReaTER** — 2025, arXiv:2503.15564. Extends GReaT with data enhancement/reduction to better encode rows.
- **CLLM (Curated LLM)** — Seedat, Huynh, van Breugel, van der Schaar, ICML 2024, arXiv:2312.12112. Prompts LLMs for tabular augmentation in low-data regimes, then curates generated rows via learning-dynamics + confidence/uncertainty metrics. *Relevance:* directly models a generator→curator loop for LLM tabular data — a template for a verifier stage in your pipeline.
- **Tabby** — 2025, arXiv:2503.02152. LLM architecture modification for tabular/structured synthesis; per-column loss tracking; captures semantic structure of unseen column values.
- **LLM-TabFlow / LLM-TabLogic** — 2025, arXiv:2503.02161. LLM infers inter-column logical/causal groupings from serialized column names+descriptions, then latent diffusion generates data preserving dependencies. Related: TAPTAP, HARMONIC, AIGT (metadata/schema-prompted generation).
- **TabuLa-8B / T4** — Gardner et al., 2024, arXiv:2406.12031. Large-scale LLM fine-tuning on ~3M tables (T4, derived from TabLib) for tabular ICL; excellent very-low-data performance.
- **Surveys:** "LLMs on Tabular Data: Prediction, Generation, Understanding" (arXiv:2402.17944); "Language Modeling on Tabular Data" (arXiv:2408.10548); "A Comprehensive Survey of Synthetic Tabular Data Generation" (arXiv:2504.16506); "A Survey on Evaluating Quality and Trustworthiness in LLM-Generated Data" (arXiv:2601.17717).

### Category 4 — LLM Agents as Designers/Configurators of Generators/Simulators (for role (b) ideation)

- **Eureka** — Ma et al., ICLR 2024, arXiv:2310.12931. GPT-4 writes and evolutionarily refines reward-function *code* from environment source + task description: "In a diverse suite of 29 open-source RL environments that include 10 distinct robot morphologies, EUREKA outperforms human experts on 83% of the tasks, leading to an average normalized improvement of 52%." *Relevance:* the canonical "LLM writes generator/objective code + in-context evolutionary refinement" pattern — directly transferable to LLM-writes-SCM-config + critic loop.
- **Eurekaverse** — 2024, arXiv:2411.01775. LLM generates *environment programs* as an adaptive curriculum for quadruped parkour. *Relevance:* LLM designs an ever-changing distribution of tasks — analogous to designing a distribution of synthetic datasets.
- **Hod et al. 2504.14368** (see Cat. 1) — LLM agent writes Pyro SCM code. The single most direct precedent for LLM-as-generator-designer in the tabular/SCM setting.
- **SD-SCM / Bynum & Cho 2024**, and "Causal Inference with Hybrid LLM Synthetic Data" (arXiv:2511.00318) — LLM specifies structural equations given a topological order to simulate (counterfactual) data. *Relevance:* confirms LLMs can author SCM mechanisms, though for causal inference not TFM priors.
- **MachineLearningLM** — 2025, arXiv:2509.06806. LoRA-continued-pretrains an LLM on millions of SCM-based synthetic tabular tasks (à la TabICL) so the LLM does many-shot ICL. *Relevance:* here the LLM is the *student* trained on a programmatic SCM prior — the inverse of your setup; useful contrast.

### Category 5 — Semantics and Real-World Knowledge in Tabular Learning

- **TabLLM** — Hegselmann et al., AISTATS 2023, arXiv:2210.10723. Serializes rows to text ("The [column] is [value]"); very-few-shot classification leverages semantic priors of column names/values; gains depend on semantically meaningful column/value text (weak when features are e.g. gene names the LLM never saw).
- **LIFT** — Dinh et al., NeurIPS 2022. Language-interfaced fine-tuning of GPT-3/GPT-J for tabular tasks; roughly comparable to classical solutions.
- **CARTE** — Kim, Grinsztajn, Varoquaux, ICML 2024. Graph-attentional pretraining across diverse sources modeling table semantics; SOTA on the semantically rich CARTE benchmark (requires task-specific fine-tuning).
- **ConTextTab** — Spinaci et al., 2025, arXiv:2506.10707 (OpenReview MmKuX9ZvM3). Semantics-aware table-native ICL learner with modality-specific embeddings (text/date/numeric) and column-header encoding, trained on real data; sets a new standard on the CARTE benchmark. *Relevance:* states your motivating gap outright — column names and semantically meaningful values are not used by TabPFN/TabICL.
- **TabSTAR** — 2025, arXiv:2505.18125 — TFM with text fields; **TARTE / Table Foundation Models** — arXiv:2505.14415 — knowledge pre-training using strings/column names; **TabGemma** — arXiv:2511.03570 — text-based tabular ICL via LLM continued pretraining + retrieval. All inject semantics via real data or real column names at inference, not via a synthetic prior.

### Category 6 — Evaluation Methodology

- **Downstream benchmarks:** Grinsztajn et al. (WhyTrees, 45 datasets), NeurIPS 2022; TabZilla (McElfresh et al., 176 datasets), NeurIPS 2023; TALENT (~300 datasets, Liu et al.); OpenML-CC18; TabRepo; **TabArena** (Erickson et al., living benchmark, arXiv:2506.16791) which investigated 1053 datasets used in tabular research and manually curated 51, spanning 16 model families (including 3 TFMs) over roughly 25 million trained model instances; TableShift (distribution shift); TabReD (temporal); TabArena's small-dataset regime (36/51 datasets, ≤10,000 samples) matches the TabPFN Nature target. *Relevance:* the expected transfer-evaluation protocols for a TFM prior.
- **Synthetic-data fidelity metrics:** α-precision / β-recall / authenticity (Alaa et al., ICML 2022); marginal (KS/TVD "Shape"), joint (correlation "Trend"), Wasserstein distance, Jensen-Shannon divergence, C2ST; TSTR vs TRTR utility. Surveys: "Systematic Assessment of Tabular Data Synthesis" (arXiv:2402.06806); ACM Computing Surveys review (10.1145/3704437); TabStruct — structural fidelity of tabular data (arXiv:2509.11950), directly relevant since it argues randomly-constructed SCMs "effectively reflect the structural information encoded in real-world tabular data."
- **Prior-quality → downstream link:** Mitra's G/P methodology (arXiv:2510.21204) and O'PRIOR's controlled prior-ablation protocol (arXiv:2605.18971) are the two templates for demonstrating that prior quality drives TFM performance. ScoringBench (arXiv:2603.29928) adds proper-scoring-rule evaluation for TFMs.
- **The realism debate:** *against realism mattering* — TabForestPFN (arXiv:2405.13396); *for realism/real data* — Real-TabPFN (arXiv:2507.03971), TabDPT, O'PRIOR; *nuanced (diversity > fidelity)* — Mitra.

### Category 7 — Ideation-Adjacent Work

- **Phi / "Textbooks Are All You Need"** — Gunasekar et al., 2023, arXiv:2306.11644; phi-1.5 arXiv:2309.05463. Textbook-quality synthetic data (GPT-3.5-generated) enables small models to break scaling trends; diversity of synthetic data emphasized. *Relevance:* the flagship "curated/synthetic data quality > scale" argument, directly analogous to your prior-quality thesis.
- **Demystifying Synthetic Data in LLM Pre-training** — 2025, arXiv:2510.01631 (EMNLP 2025). Systematic scaling-law study finding that pure synthetic data is *not* automatically superior to CommonCrawl. *Relevance:* a sober counterweight and a likely reviewer citation on the limits of synthetic pretraining.
- **CLLM** (Cat. 3) — generator + principled curation is a self-instruct/verifier analogue for tabular data.
- **Domain randomization / sim2real** and **PFN prior-misspecification / prior-vs-real distribution shift** — invoked conceptually as the theoretical frame for why prior *diversity* (not fidelity) may be what matters; TabForestPFN and Mitra provide the tabular-specific evidence for that frame.

### Memorization / Contamination Literature (objection-critical)
- **Elephants Never Forget: Memorization and Learning of Tabular Data in LLMs** — Bordt et al., COLM 2024, arXiv:2404.06209. Shows GPT-3.5/GPT-4 have memorized many popular tabular datasets verbatim (e.g., Iris, Wine, Adult, Housing, OpenML Diabetes, Titanic); provides four memorization tests and the interpretml/LLM-Tabular-Memorization-Checker package.
- **When Tables Leak: Attacking String Memorization in LLM-Based Tabular Data Generation** — Ward et al., 2025, arXiv:2512.08875. A no-box membership-inference attack (LevAtt) exposes privacy leakage in both fine-tuned and in-context LLM tabular generators, in some cases acting as a perfect membership classifier.
- **Detection of LLM Contamination with Tabular Data** — Ronval et al., IDA 2025. Contamination detection across GPT/Llama/Gemma/Phi families.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Lock positioning around role (b) and pre-empt the two nearest misses.** Frame the contribution as: *"An LLM agent that configures a programmatic (SCM-based) tabular generator — choosing DAG structure, column semantics, and distribution parameters — to produce a semantics-and-realism-aware pretraining prior for TFMs."* In related work, explicitly distinguish (i) **Hod et al. 2504.14368** (same LLM→SCM-code mechanism, but per-schema DP-classifier pretraining, not a TFM prior over a distribution of datasets) and (ii) **O'PRIOR 2605.18971** and **Mitra** (realistic/mixed priors for TFMs, but no LLM and no semantics). Your defensible claim: *first to use an LLM agent to design the synthetic prior of a TFM, and first to bake real-world column semantics into that synthetic prior.*

**Stage 2 — Choose the role-(a) vs role-(b) split deliberately.** Prefer role (b) as the headline (cheap, contamination-resistant, controllable, sampling done by fast code). Include role (a) — LLM directly emits rows/schemas — only as a small ablation or seeding mechanism, and address memorization/cost head-on. Threshold to change the decision: if role-(a) generation cost approaches the compute of downstream TFM pretraining, or if memorization tests fire (Stage 4), demote role (a) to schema/seed-only.

**Stage 3 — Evaluation plan the reviewers will expect.** (a) Pretrain a fixed TFM architecture (reuse TabPFNv2 or TabICL code) on your LLM-designed prior; hold architecture/compute constant and vary only the prior (O'PRIOR protocol). (b) Downstream: WhyTrees/Grinsztajn (45) + TabZilla (176) + TALENT (~300) + TabArena (51 curated); report against TabPFN v2, TabICL/TabICLv2, Mitra, TabForestPFN, and, if feasible, Real-TabPFN/TabDPT. (c) Characterize your prior with Mitra's diversity/distinctiveness/performance framework (G matrix, P vector). (d) Report synthetic-data fidelity (α-precision/β-recall, Wasserstein, C2ST) to quantify "realism," then correlate realism with downstream gain — this directly adjudicates the realism debate for your prior.

**Stage 4 — Run and report a contamination/memorization audit.** Use the interpretml LLM-Tabular-Memorization-Checker and cite Bordt et al. (arXiv:2404.06209) and Ward et al. (arXiv:2512.08875). Threshold: if the LLM can reconstruct verbatim rows of any evaluation dataset (Iris/Wine/Adult/Diabetes/Titanic are known-memorized), route generation through role (b) (schema+SCM only, no verbatim values) and/or exclude those datasets from evaluation — and state this explicitly to defuse the objection preemptively.

**Stage 5 — Take an explicit position in the realism debate.** Based on the literature, the likely defensible thesis is *conditional*: semantic/realistic priors should help most in low-data, semantically rich, zero-shot settings (where world knowledge substitutes for data), whereas complexity/diversity alone may suffice after fine-tuning (TabForestPFN) or when semantics are absent (TabArena's non-semantic datasets). Design at least one experiment that isolates this boundary (e.g., CARTE-CLF semantically rich vs TabArena non-semantic splits, zero-shot vs fine-tuned).

## Caveats
- **Nearest-miss risk:** Hod et al. (2504.14368, Apr 2025) is close enough on method that a reviewer will cite it; distinguish it in the first pass of related work, not bury it.
- **"Realism may not matter" objection is live and evidence-backed** (TabForestPFN): do not merely assert real-world-mimicking priors are better — show *when* semantics/realism helps versus when complexity/diversity alone suffices.
- **Synthetic-pretraining skepticism** (Demystifying Synthetic Data, arXiv:2510.01631): synthetic data is not automatically superior; frame gains carefully and benchmark against strong real-data baselines (Real-TabPFN/TabDPT).
- **Cost at scale:** TFM pretraining consumes millions of synthetic datasets; direct LLM row-generation at that scale is likely economically infeasible — a further reason role (b) (LLM configures, program samples) is the sound architecture.
- **Fast-moving 2026 preprints:** several cited works (O'PRIOR 2605.18971, TabICLv2 2602.11139, Zhu & Ler 2605.09518, Ward et al. 2512.08875, LimiX-2M 2606.04485) are recent/future-dated arXiv preprints; verify final venues/versions before citation.
- **Residual search risk:** exhaustive OpenReview submission crawling was not performed; a small chance remains of an unindexed workshop paper closer to the idea. The "no direct competitor" verdict is robust across arXiv/Semantic Scholar as of July 2026 but should be re-checked at submission time.
- **Terminology traps:** APT's "agents" are adversarial data generators, and "Causal Agent" (arXiv:2408.06849) is an LLM that *analyzes* existing tables — neither is an LLM-agent-designs-prior competitor; disambiguate to avoid a reviewer conflating them.
